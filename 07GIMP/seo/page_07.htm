Think long term when registering domains

Register your site for 5-10 years or longer

Although not mentioned often this could become more important in the future. In 2005 Google applied for a patent that uses the expiration date of the domain name as one of their ranking criteria. The next time you purchase or renew your domain name, extend the date for 5-10 years. This gives your site much more legitimacy than if the domain is only around for another year or two.

As domain names become more and more scarce (and more and more valuable) this makes even more sense.

What happens if your domain registration doesn't get renewed?

The industry is filled with stories of companies not getting their domain re-registered for some reason or other. People around the world watch the expiration lists hoping to pick up a valuable domain name that can later be resold, sometimes for tens of thousands of dollars.

When your business loses its domain name you can no longer get email and your customers will see someone else's web page (or no web page) until you get it back.
