The Connection

Next, you need to connect this browser/client to the Internet. This is done either by a

-- Dial-up networking (slow - 56kbps),

-- DSL (Digital Subscriber Line) (faster - 512 kbps),

-- wireless, or

-- a cable modem (fastest - 1,500 kbps or 1.5mbps).

The dial-up connection consists of a modem that converts the digital information from your computer into an audio signal used by the telephone line. Another modem at the ISP converts the audio signal back to digital so the request can be sent across the Internet which can only handle digital signals (ones and zeros) like your computer.

The DSL and cable modems connect to a network card in your computer use TCP/IP digital signals without the added overhead of converting and de-converting the signals into audio signals.
