The Essential Tags

Here are the essential elements for every web page.

[D]<html> The entire page is framed using <html> and </html> . Everything before and after these tags is ignored by the browser.

<head> The head tag holds the title elements as well as the <meta />, <script>, and <style> elements that you will be learning about later in the course.

<title> For now, the only thing you will be putting in the <head> section will be the <title> </title>. Everything between these two tags will display in the title bar of the browser window. Important! Don't confuse this with titles on the page. The text inside these two tags only displays in the title bar and in the Start bar at the bottom of Windows.

</title> This marks the end of the title section.

</head> And this marks the end of the head section.

<body> This starts the beginning of the body of the web page and will hold 99% of your code. Everything inside this element will display as the actual web page.

</body> This tells the browser that it is at the end of the body section. Remember, every start element must have a matching stop element. If you leave off the </body> tag the client's browser just may go drifting off to Planet Ten and never display the contents of your web page!

</html> And, last, but not least, there's the matching stop tag for the opening <html>. Forget this stop tag and you may have client's sending you nasty email about blank web pages. If you are getting paid by the hour, your clients' will wonder what they paid you for.
