Secret Codes

There are three types of tags or secret codes in HTML.

They are double elements, the single elements, and the special characters.

Double Elements

First, the most common type: double tag elements. Each start tag has a matching stop tag. Here are some examples:

  • The bold element: <strong> and </strong>
  • The paragraph element: <p> and </p>
  • The comment element:
  • <!-- and -->

Notice with the comment tag that there is a ! in the start and not in the stop element. Also both tags must have two dashes.

Single Elements

Next, a little less common, are the single elements. These only have one tag. They end with a space slash " /" to tell the browser to stop. Here are some examples:

  • The horizontal rule element: <hr />
  • The image element: <img />
  • The line return or break element: <br />

Special Characters

And finally, the special characters. These are used to display characters that aren't available on the regular keyboard. Here are some examples:

  • a non-blank space: &nbsp; which will act as a place holder
  • a bullet: &middot; or &#149;
  • a copyright symbol: &copy; or &#169;
  • the less than sign: &lt; which is the less than symbol < and is used to display HTML tags (If you just use the < the browser will be read it and discard it as a tag.)

Notice that the special characters each begin with an & and end with a semi-colon ";"

The browser pays attention to the & just like it does with the < .

You may notice that some textbooks and older code out on the web do not include the / for the one-sided. That is the old HTML way of doing things. However, when you use HTML you have to have a matching stop tag for every start tag. (This is one of those XML rules.) To meet this criteria simply include a / at the end of the single tag. (You have to include the space so old browser that don't understand HTML don't cough and spit on a "bad" element, such as <br/> and then ignore it.)
