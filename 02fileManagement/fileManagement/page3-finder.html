Finder has two panels.

On the left is a list of directories (also called folders) and drives.

On the right is a list of the directories/folders and files located inside the item that is selected in the left-side panel.

You can easily click and drag files and folders around to organize your files. Just watch carefully so you don't drop your files in the wrong folder!

You can change the view by clicking on the List icon located in the top right corner of the Explorer window.

You can use the "find" field to locate files with the same name or part of a name. (Right hand corner. Look for the magnifying glass.)

Notice the text along the bottom of the screen. It shows the "path" the computer uses to find the file. This is especially helpful when a file is store inside a long series of folders and subfolders.

There are four different view icons along the top menu bar. The most useful is the multi-column view. It shows multiple layers of folders inside of folders and the files they contain.

The image icon allows you to see the contents of each individual file. This is very useful when looking at a directory that contains graphic image files.
